YouTube Thumbnail Size and Specs

Upload a thumbnail that's the wrong size and YouTube either rejects it or crops it awkwardly. Get the dimensions, format, and file-size limit right the first time, and your custom thumbnail looks crisp everywhere — from a phone feed to a TV.

The exact specs

SpecRequirement
Dimensions1280 × 720 px (recommended)
Minimum width640 px
Aspect ratio16:9
File formatsJPG, PNG, WebP, GIF
Maximum file size2 MB

The single most important number is 1280 × 720 px at a 16:9 ratio. That matches YouTube's player exactly, so nothing gets cropped, and it scales down cleanly for the small previews that make up most of a viewer's screen.

Why 16:9 matters so much

YouTube displays your thumbnail in a 16:9 frame everywhere — search results, the home feed, suggested videos, and the player itself. If you upload a square or 4:3 image, YouTube pads or crops it to fit, often slicing off your title text or cutting your subject in half. Designing at 1280 × 720 from the start guarantees the whole image survives. If you need to fix an off-ratio image, see how to resize an image online.

Staying under the 2 MB limit

Detailed thumbnails — especially PNGs with lots of color and text — can easily exceed YouTube's 2 MB cap. Two easy fixes:

  • Compress it. Export as JPG at around 85% quality, which keeps text sharp while dropping well under 2 MB. Our compression guide walks through the settings.
  • Convert a heavy PNG. If your design tool exported a large PNG, convert PNG to JPG or to WebP for a much smaller file at the same resolution.

Design tips that survive the small preview

Most viewers see your thumbnail at a fraction of its full size — sometimes barely larger than a postage stamp on a phone. Keep faces large, limit on-image text to three or four big words, and use high contrast so it reads at a glance. Save your master file at full 1280 × 720 resolution; never design tiny and upscale, because enlarging a small image blurs it (more on that in the resize guide).

JPG, PNG, or WebP for a thumbnail?

JPG is the safe, universal choice and compresses photos well. PNG keeps text and flat graphics razor-sharp but produces bigger files. WebP gives you the best of both — sharp and small — and YouTube accepts it. If you're unsure, our best image format guide compares them directly.

Frequently asked questions

What is the correct YouTube thumbnail size?

1280 × 720 pixels with a 16:9 aspect ratio. The minimum acceptable width is 640 px, but 1280 × 720 is recommended so the image stays sharp at every display size.

What is the maximum thumbnail file size?

2 MB. If your file is larger, compress it as JPG at about 85% quality or convert a heavy PNG to JPG or WebP to get under the limit.

Can I use a PNG for my YouTube thumbnail?

Yes. YouTube accepts JPG, PNG, WebP, and GIF. PNG keeps text crisp but is larger, so watch the 2 MB limit — converting to JPG or WebP usually helps.
