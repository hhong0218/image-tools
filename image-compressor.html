What is image compression?

Image compression shrinks a file's size, usually by re-encoding the pixel data more efficiently or discarding detail that's hard to notice. This tool re-encodes your image at an adjustable quality level using the browser's Canvas API, so you can trade a little visual detail for a much smaller file — ideal for faster websites, email attachments, and upload limits.

How to compress an image online

  • Drop your JPG, PNG, or WebP into the box above (or click to browse).
  • Drag the quality slider — lower means a smaller file but more visible compression artifacts. Watch the live preview to find the sweet spot.
  • Click Compress image, check the before/after size, then hit Download.

Why doesn't PNG shrink as much?

PNG is a lossless format — it's not designed to throw away detail the way JPG and WebP are. Because of that, the browser's canvas.toBlob() quality parameter has little to no effect on PNG output; any size reduction you see comes from re-encoding and color/palette optimization, not from a quality trade-off. If you need a much smaller file and don't need transparency, converting to JPG or WebP (which does support transparency) will almost always beat compressing the PNG itself.

Frequently asked questions

Is my image uploaded anywhere?

No. Compression happens 100% locally using an HTML <canvas> and canvas.toBlob() in your browser. Nothing is uploaded, there's no account, and no image ever leaves your device.

Which format compresses best?

WebP usually gives the smallest file at a given visual quality, followed by JPG. PNG compresses the least here since it's lossless by design — see above.

Will compressing reduce image quality?

For JPG and WebP, yes — that's the trade-off. Keep the slider as high as your target file size allows, and use the live preview to judge whether the result still looks good enough for your use.
