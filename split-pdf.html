Extract or separate PDF pages — privately

Got a long PDF but only need a few pages? This split PDF tool lets you pull out a page range into a fresh document, or break a file into single-page PDFs bundled in a ZIP. It runs entirely on your device with the open-source pdf-lib library, so your document never leaves your browser — no upload, no account, no waiting.

How to split a PDF

  • Drop your PDF into the box above (or click to browse).
  • Choose Extract a page range and type the pages you want (like 1-3, 5, 8-10), or pick Split every page to get one PDF per page.
  • Click Split PDF — your new PDF (or a ZIP of single-page PDFs) downloads instantly.

Frequently asked questions

Are my PDF files uploaded?

No. The file is read and split entirely in your browser with the open-source pdf-lib library. Nothing is uploaded — there's no server and no account. It works offline once the page has loaded.

How do I write the page range?

Use page numbers separated by commas, and dashes for ranges. For example, 1-3, 5, 8-10 extracts pages 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 9, and 10 in that order. Leave the box blank to keep all pages.

Does splitting reduce quality?

No. Pages are copied intact, so text, images, and vector graphics are preserved exactly. Splitting only selects and re-packages pages; it doesn't re-render or recompress them.

Can I split a password-protected PDF?

Encrypted PDFs must be unlocked first. Remove the password in your PDF viewer, then split the unlocked copy here.
