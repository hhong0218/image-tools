How to remove a background

  • Drop a photo of a person into the box above (or click to browse).
  • Click Remove Background. The first run downloads a small AI model; after that it's instant to start.
  • Check the cut-out on the checkerboard preview, then hit Download PNG to save a transparent image.

What this tool is good at (and what it isn't)

This remover uses MODNet, an open-source portrait-matting model. It's built to separate people from their surroundings, so it shines on headshots, selfies, profile pictures, and full-body shots. It is not a general object cut-out tool — for products, pets, or arbitrary objects the edges may be rough, because the model was trained to find people. Honest expectations: great for portraits, hit-or-miss for everything else.

Are my photos uploaded anywhere?

No. Both the AI model and your photo are processed entirely in your browser using ONNX Runtime Web (WebAssembly). There is no server, no account, and no tracking of your images. The model file is served from this site and cached by your browser — you can even go offline after the first run and it still works.

Frequently asked questions

Why does the first run take a moment?

The first time you use it, your browser downloads the ~7 MB matting model and the WebAssembly runtime. Both are then cached, so later runs start immediately. Segmentation itself takes a couple of seconds on a typical laptop.

The edges around hair look soft — is that normal?

Matting hair is genuinely hard. MODNet produces a soft alpha edge, which usually looks natural on a new background. Well-lit photos with good contrast between the person and the background give the cleanest results.

Can I remove the background from a product or an animal?

You can try, but this model is trained on people, so non-human subjects often come out rough. It's meant as a portrait background remover.

What license is the model under?

The MODNet model is Apache-2.0 and the ONNX Runtime is MIT — both permit free and commercial use. Full notices ship with the site.
