Why convert HEIC to JPG?

Since iOS 11, iPhones and iPads save photos as HEIC (High Efficiency Image Container) to fit more pictures in less space. The catch: Windows, many websites, older apps, and most editors can't open HEIC. Converting to JPG gives you a universally-compatible file you can view, upload, email, or edit anywhere — while keeping great quality.

How to convert HEIC to JPG

  • Drop your .HEIC photo into the box above (or click to browse). On Windows you may need to type *.heic or choose "All files" in the picker.
  • Pick a JPG quality — 92% keeps the photo crisp. Click Convert to JPG. Large photos take a few seconds while they decode locally.
  • Check the preview and file size, then hit Download JPG.

Frequently asked questions

Are my photos uploaded anywhere?

No. Decoding happens entirely in your browser using the open-source libheif engine (compiled to WebAssembly). Your photo never leaves your device — there's no server, no account, and no tracking. You can even go offline after the page loads.

What is a HEIC file?

HEIC is Apple's container for HEIF images. It stores the same photo at roughly half the size of JPG, which is why iPhones use it — but that efficiency comes at the cost of compatibility outside the Apple ecosystem.

Will I lose quality?

JPG is lossy, so there's some compression, but at 90%+ the difference is hard to see. Keep the slider high if the photo matters; lower it only to shrink the file further.

Can I convert several HEIC photos at once?

This page converts one photo at a time. Batch conversion is on the way — for now, convert and download each one, or browse the main image tools.
