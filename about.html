About ImageTools

ImageTools is a free, privacy-first toolbox for the everyday image jobs — resizing, compressing, converting formats, turning iPhone HEIC photos into JPG, and stripping EXIF metadata — all in one fast, no-nonsense site with no signup and no clutter.

Privacy first, by design

Every tool on ImageTools runs entirely in your web browser. When you drop in a photo to resize, compress, convert, or clean, that image is processed locally on your own device using standard browser technology — the Canvas API and WebAssembly. Nothing you open is uploaded to or stored on a server. There is no account, no cloud storage, and no copy of your file kept anywhere. In fact, once a tool's page has loaded, you can disconnect from the internet and it will keep working. That means you can work with private, sensitive images without them ever leaving your machine.

How it works

The site is a collection of fast, self-contained static pages. Each tool decodes and re-encodes your image right in the browser: resizing and compression use the Canvas API, HEIC decoding uses the open-source libheif engine compiled to WebAssembly, and EXIF removal simply re-writes the pixels without the embedded metadata. There are no heavy frameworks and no third-party trackers embedded in the tools themselves. We use Google Analytics for anonymous, aggregated usage statistics and Google AdSense to display ads that keep the site free; both are described in our Privacy Policy.

The core promise: your images never leave your device. There is no upload, no server-side processing, and no retention. When you close the tab, nothing remains. See our Terms of Service for the full details.

What's inside

ImageTools currently includes:

  • Image Resizer — resize by pixels or percentage while keeping the aspect ratio.
  • Image Compressor — shrink JPG, PNG, and WebP files with a live quality preview.
  • HEIC to JPG — convert iPhone and iPad photos into a universally compatible format.
  • Remove EXIF — strip location and camera metadata before you share a photo.
  • Image to PDF — combine images into a single PDF document.
  • Format converters — PNG to JPG, JPG to PNG, WebP to JPG, and more.
