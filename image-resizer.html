How to resize an image online

Resizing changes an image's pixel dimensions — handy for meeting an upload limit, fitting a profile photo, or preparing a picture for social media. With the tool above:

  • Drop your image in (or click to browse), then pick a preset for a common social size, or switch to manual Pixels or Percentage mode.
  • In Pixels mode, type a new width or height and keep Lock aspect ratio on so the image scales proportionally without stretching.
  • Click Resize image, check the preview, then hit Download.

Tip: shrinking is safe, but enlarging an image beyond its original resolution just stretches existing pixels — it won't add real detail.

Common social media image sizes

  • Instagram post (square) — 1080 × 1080px
  • Twitter / X post — 1200 × 675px (16:9)
  • Facebook cover photo — 820 × 312px (desktop)

These are stable, widely-used specs — use the preset buttons above to jump straight to them.

Frequently asked questions

Is my image uploaded to resize it?

No. Resizing happens 100% locally on an HTML <canvas> in your browser. Nothing is uploaded, and no image ever leaves your device.

Will resizing reduce quality?

Shrinking an image is visually lossless — you're just scaling pixels down. Enlarging beyond the original size will look softer, since there's no extra detail to add.

What happens if I don't lock the aspect ratio?

The image will stretch or squash to exactly match the width and height you enter, which can distort faces and straight lines. Leave Lock aspect ratio on unless you specifically want a different ratio.
