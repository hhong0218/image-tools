Combine PDFs into one file — privately

Need to bundle a cover letter, résumé, and references into one document? Or stitch together scanned pages that came out as separate files? This merge PDF tool joins any number of PDFs into a single file, in the exact order you arrange them. Everything happens locally with the open-source pdf-lib library, so your documents never leave your device — no server, no account, no waiting on an upload.

How to merge PDF files

  • Drop your PDF files into the box above (or click to browse). Add as many as you need.
  • Reorder them with the ▲▼ buttons so the pages appear in the sequence you want.
  • Click Merge PDF — the combined file downloads instantly as merged.pdf.

Frequently asked questions

Are my PDF files uploaded?

No. The files are read and combined entirely in your browser with the open-source pdf-lib library. Nothing is uploaded — there's no server and no account. You can even go offline after the page loads and it still works.

Is there a limit on how many PDFs I can merge?

There's no hard limit. Because the work happens on your device, very large documents simply use more memory and take a moment longer to assemble.

Will merging change the quality of my pages?

No. Pages are copied intact — text, images, and vector graphics are preserved exactly. Merging only rearranges pages into one file; it doesn't re-render or recompress them.

Can I merge password-protected PDFs?

Encrypted PDFs need to be unlocked first. If a file is password-protected, remove the protection in your PDF viewer, then merge the unlocked copy here.
